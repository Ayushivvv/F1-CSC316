About the Project

Our project explores the world of Formula One (F1) through the lens of data. Since its inception in 1950, F1 has hosted over 1,142 World Championship events across 76 seasons, 34 countries, and 77 circuits. Each event generates a wealth of information — from categorical data like drivers, teams, and locations to quantitative measures like lap times, points earned, and pit stop counts.

Leveraging these datasets, we aim to answer the central question: What Makes an F1 Champion? By analyzing differences between teams and drivers, as well as race conditions and strategies, we uncover the factors that define success.
